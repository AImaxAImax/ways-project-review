# Research notes — mantis_shrimp_cavitation_punch

## Status
Gate 1 packet prepared. No credits spent. Rendering not started.

## Core fact
Smasher mantis shrimp strikes are fast enough to create cavitation: vapor bubbles form near the appendage/prey and then collapse, adding damaging force after the initial mechanical strike.

## Verified/source-backed claims to use
- "cavitation bubbles form and collapse near the prey" — supported by Nature article text available in page metadata/snippets for DOI 10.1038/428819a.
- "elastic energy powers the punch" — supported by JEB/Inside JEB DOI 10.1242/jeb.040691.
- "kinematics and cavitation can be modeled/tested in a physical striking device" — supported by Bioinspiration & Biomimetics DOI 10.1088/1748-3182/9/1/016014.

## Claims to avoid or qualify
- Do not say the shrimp "boils water" as if it is heating the water.
- Do not say the flash is fire, electricity, plasma, or light from the animal.
- Do not promise a visible natural "flash" in every source clip; the hook can refer to a shock/cavitation moment, but the visuals need literal bubbles/impact or a clearly caption-carried macro shot.
