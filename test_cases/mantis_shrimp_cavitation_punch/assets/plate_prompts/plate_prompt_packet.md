# Plate prompt packet — mantis_shrimp_cavitation_punch

## Shared WAYS style
Vertical 9:16, premium documentary macro, cinematic lighting, family-safe educational wonder, no text, no signs, no logos, no UI, clean caption-safe negative space, realistic animal anatomy, phone-size readable subject.

## Hard negatives
fake text, labels, diagrams, UI overlays, extra limbs, rubber toy shrimp, fantasy monster, gore, blood, aggressive horror teeth, blurry subject, cluttered reef, unreadable impact, bottom captions, watermark, logo.

## Shot prompts

### shot01_hook_flash
Photoreal macro of a peacock mantis shrimp at the moment its club strikes near a small shell underwater, tiny bright cavitation bubbles and shock ripples around the impact, shrimp anatomy realistic, subject large in frame, clean dark-blue water background, cinematic high-speed photography feel, no text.

### shot02_tiny_animal_scale
Documentary macro portrait of a real mantis shrimp partly out of a burrow, colorful but natural, eyes and folded raptorial appendages visible, scale feels small not monster-sized, clean negative space for centered captions, no text.

### shot03_club_motion
Close-up of mantis shrimp raptorial club moving forward underwater, motion implied with bubbles and water distortion, realistic anatomy, no extra appendages, no text.

### shot04_cavitation_bubbles
Premium underwater macro of a small collapsing bubble cloud near a strike point, no animal if necessary, dark teal water, crisp bubble edges, scientific but not diagrammatic, no labels or text.

### shot05_second_hit
Cinematic macro impact aftermath: bubble cloud collapsing beside a shell/prey proxy, water ripples and suspended particles, no gore, no text, phone-readable focal point.

### shot06_final_water_punches_too
Wide-ish macro composition of mantis shrimp facing the strike zone with bubbles trailing from the impact, heroic but real-documentary, enough top/middle negative space for final caption, no text.

## Gate 2 notes
- If animal anatomy is below 9/10, reject the plate even if the bubble/impact looks good.
- If cavitation reads only because of a fake label or diagram, reject.
- If the source is a real mantis photo but static, it can pass as a plate only if the later motion lane has a believable plan.
