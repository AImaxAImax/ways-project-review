# Test Short 001 — Sharks Are Older Than Trees

## Working title
**Sharks Are Older Than Trees**

## Channel positioning test
Curious, parent-approved science facts for kids/families. Smart screen time, not toddler slop.

## Target length
35–45 seconds, vertical 1080×1920.

## Core thesis
Sharks are an ancient design: they appeared before forests existed.

## Voice direction
Warm, curious, trustworthy, slightly magical. More “calm science storyteller” than “hyper kids host.” Avoid babyish delivery.

## Final script
Sharks are older than trees.

That sounds impossible, but it’s true.

The first sharks appeared over four hundred million years ago.

Trees came later, around three hundred and fifty million years ago.

That means sharks were already swimming through ancient oceans before forests existed.

So next time you see a shark, remember: you’re looking at a living design older than the trees in your backyard.

## Visual style target
Soft 3D / premium illustrated educational animation. Rich but clean. Parent-trusted, not uncanny.

Style keywords:
- soft cinematic 3D educational animation
- natural science museum exhibit
- warm volumetric lighting
- kid-friendly but not childish
- clear silhouettes
- no gore, no scary teeth focus
- no readable text inside generated scenes unless specifically requested

## Acceptance criteria for first test
- Sounds trustworthy, not creepy.
- Hook lands in first 1.5 seconds.
- Visuals are clear on mobile.
- No overly scary shark imagery.
- Captions readable.
- Can be produced repeatedly without heroic effort.
