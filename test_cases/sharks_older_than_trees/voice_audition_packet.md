# Voice Audition Packet — Sharks Older Than Trees

## Decision
Reject the current proxy voices. Do not use Edge/local default TTS to judge final channel quality.

The voice needs to feel like a real human narrator, not a synthetic explainer. If the voice is wrong, the whole channel reads as low-quality kids AI content even if the script is solid.

## Target voice
A warm adult narrator with a natural, conversational read.

Feels like:
- older sibling telling you a weird fact
- calm science storyteller
- curious, amused, trustworthy
- parent-safe without sounding childish
- premium YouTube / podcast adjacent

Does not feel like:
- AI educational channel
- TikTok fact voice
- corporate training narrator
- fake documentary bass voice
- kids cartoon character
- overacted bedtime story

## Performance direction
Read it like you genuinely just learned the fact and are quietly delighted by it.

- First line: say it like a secret, not an announcement.
- “Seriously.” should be dry and amused.
- Squirrel line can have a tiny smile.
- “four hundred million years ago” should slow down slightly.
- Final line should land with quiet awe, not drama.

## Script
Sharks were here before trees.

Seriously.

Before forests. Before apples. Before squirrels had anywhere to hide nuts.

The first sharks showed up more than four hundred million years ago.

Trees took their time. They didn’t really arrive until tens of millions of years later.

So when you look at a shark, you’re not just looking at an animal.

You’re looking at a survivor from a world before forests.

## Auditions to generate
Generate these as separate reads, not one blended attempt:

1. **Warm older brother / uncle**
   - Natural male voice
   - Medium pitch, not deep trailer voice
   - Slightly amused, calm, intimate

2. **Warm older sister / aunt**
   - Natural female voice
   - Clear, gentle, not teacher-ish
   - Curious and lightly playful

3. **Soft science storyteller**
   - Could be male or female
   - More documentary/podcast, but still casual
   - Slow enough to feel premium, not sleepy

4. **Younger curious narrator**
   - Slightly more energy
   - Still not TikTok, not Disney Channel
   - Best if the channel skews kid/family

## Acceptance test
A voice passes only if:
- It sounds human enough that the visuals can be judged fairly.
- It does not trigger “AI channel” instincts in the first five seconds.
- A parent would not find it annoying in the room.
- The final line feels earned, not fake profound.

## Current status
Local Edge voices are acceptable only for timing scratch tracks. They are not acceptable for final concept validation.
