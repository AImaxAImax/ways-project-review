# Visual Style Bible v4 — Consistent Style, Different Settings

## Direction
Use one locked art language across the whole short, but make every shot a different *place* and *idea*.

**Style lock:** premium handmade stop-motion diorama / layered cut-paper + soft clay miniature look. Rounded shapes, matte paper textures, shallow depth of field, warm museum-lighting, teal/amber palette, kid-safe natural-history tone.

**Do not lock by repeating the shark.** Lock by repeating materials, lighting, palette, lens, and composition polish.

## Non-negotiable rule
**Say dog, see dog.** If the narration says trees, show trees or their absence. If it says apples, show apples. If it says squirrels/nuts, show squirrel/nut gag. If it says 400 million years, show time/strata/fossils. Do not make every line a shark underwater.

## Setting separation
1. **Before trees:** split ancient ocean + barren alien land. Shark may appear, but land/no-trees must dominate.
2. **Seriously:** natural-history museum proof table/exhibit. Fossil/display setting dominates.
3. **Before forests/apples/squirrels:** comic prehistoric land gag. Apple + squirrel + acorn/nut must be visible as simple props/icons.
4. **400+ million years:** vertical geologic timeline / rock layers / fossil clock. Time must dominate.
5. **Trees arrived later:** primitive early forest shoreline. Plants/trees dominate.
6. **Not just an animal:** modern aquarium/museum viewer POV. Human silhouette dominates.
7. **Survivor from world before forests:** final quiet ancient-ocean awe. This is the one shark-led emotional shot.

## Prompting formula
`LOCKED_STYLE + SHOT_SETTING + CLEAR_SUBJECTS + COMPOSITION_RULE + no readable text`

## Rejection criteria
- More than two frames look like generic underwater shark portraits.
- The apple/squirrel gag lacks apple, squirrel, or acorn/nut.
- The timeline is just an ocean scene.
- Early forest shot does not clearly feature primitive trees/plants.
- Modern viewer shot lacks a person/viewer/aquarium/museum context.
- Generated text/letters/logos appear inside image.
