# Script revisions — less AI, more human

## What was wrong with v1
- Too clean and explanatory.
- Every line had the same “fact explainer” rhythm.
- It sounded like a chatbot summary, not a writer with taste.
- The final line was okay, but too polished in an AI way.
- No tension, no little turn, no human phrasing.

## Voice target
Calm, curious, slightly conspiratorial. Like an older sibling telling you a weird fact they genuinely love.

Not “educational content voice.”
Not “HEY KIDS.”
Not “nature documentary narrator trying too hard.”

## Version A — tight and natural, recommended
Sharks were here before trees.

Seriously.

Before forests. Before apples. Before squirrels had anywhere to hide nuts.

The first sharks showed up more than four hundred million years ago.

Trees took their time. They didn’t really arrive until tens of millions of years later.

So when you look at a shark, you’re not just looking at an animal.

You’re looking at a survivor from a world before forests.

## Version B — more kid/family playful
Sharks are older than trees.

Which sounds fake.

But it isn’t.

Sharks were already swimming around more than four hundred million years ago.

Back then, Earth didn’t have forests like the ones we know today.

No tall trees. No shady woods. No squirrels yelling from branches.

Just ancient oceans, strange fish, and sharks already figuring things out.

That’s why sharks feel so perfectly built.

They’ve had a very, very long time to practice.

## Version C — more cinematic
Long before the first forest, there were sharks.

No oak trees. No pine trees. No birds in the branches.

Just ancient oceans, full of strange little creatures, and the early shapes of sharks moving through the dark.

That was more than four hundred million years ago.

Trees came later.

A lot later.

So the next time you see a shark, remember this: that animal comes from a design older than the forest itself.

## My recommendation
Use **Version A** for the first real test.

It has the strongest hook, less “school report” energy, and the final line gives the Short a little soul:

> You’re looking at a survivor from a world before forests.

That line feels memorable without sounding like AI motivational copy.
