# WAYS Topic Queue: Batch 2 (20 ideas)

**Screened against the live data (2026-06-07).** Every topic here fits the proven winner pattern from shark / frog / shrimp:

- One counterintuitive fact about a **recognizable** animal or natural phenomenon (strong visual prior).
- Cold-open hook that states the claim in the first 2 seconds.
- The fact can be **shown literally** with footage of the subject, not explained with a cutaway diagram.
- No process/internal-anatomy beat as the core visual. That is the wombat failure mode (43.87% avg-viewed, throttled at 133 views).

**Lane tags:**

- `WAN` = strong-prior subject, animates cleanly in Wan2.2 I2V. Safest. Fill the queue with these first.
- `MIXED` = mostly WAN, one effect/payoff beat that may need a light motion-graphic or compositing touch.
- `MG` = natural phenomenon or scale fact that needs the motion-graphics lane. Higher risk. Use these to deliberately test the MG lane on one cohort, not to anchor the channel.

Each card maps to your Stage A `idea_card.json` schema. `slug`, `hook`, `core_fact`, `lane`, `visual` (how to show it), `risk`.

## Lane A: Safe WAN (16) — fill the queue with these

### 1. octopus_three_hearts

- **hook:** “An octopus has three hearts. And blue blood.”
- **core_fact:** Octopuses have three hearts (two gill, one systemic) and copper-based hemocyanin makes their blood blue.
- **lane:** WAN
- **visual:** Live octopus, color/texture shifts, close eye contact. Caption carries the number.
- **risk:** Low. Do not attempt an internal-heart cutaway; show the animal and let the caption state the fact.

### 2. immortal_jellyfish

- **hook:** “This jellyfish can skip death.”
- **core_fact:** Turritopsis dohrnii can revert from adult back to its polyp stage, effectively restarting its life cycle.
- **lane:** WAN
- **visual:** Drifting translucent jellyfish, glow, slow pulse. Strong loop-the-hook potential.
- **risk:** Low.

### 3. axolotl_regrows

- **hook:** “This animal can regrow its own brain.”
- **core_fact:** Axolotls regenerate limbs, spinal cord, heart tissue, and parts of their brain.
- **lane:** WAN
- **visual:** Pink axolotl, gilly frills, smiling face. Very photogenic, strong prior.
- **risk:** Low. Show the animal, not a regrowth time-lapse diagram.

### 4. sloth_breath

- **hook:** “A sloth can hold its breath longer than a dolphin.”
- **core_fact:** By slowing its heart rate, a sloth can suppress breathing for up to ~40 minutes, beating a dolphin’s typical dive.
- **lane:** WAN
- **visual:** Sloth moving slowly through canopy, underwater sloth swim if available.
- **risk:** Low.

### 5. flamingo_pink

- **hook:** “Flamingos are not born pink.”
- **core_fact:** Flamingo chicks are grey; the pink comes from carotenoid pigments in the shrimp and algae they eat.
- **lane:** WAN
- **visual:** Grey chick beat, then vivid pink adult beat. The color shift IS the payoff. Two clean stills.
- **risk:** Low. Strong say-dog-see-dog.

### 6. crow_grudge

- **hook:** “Crows remember your face. And they hold grudges.”
- **core_fact:** Crows recognize individual human faces and can remember a threatening person for years, even warning other crows.
- **lane:** WAN
- **visual:** Crow tilting head, sharp eye contact, group of crows. Slightly menacing-but-kid-safe.
- **risk:** Low.

### 7. narwhal_tooth

- **hook:** “A narwhal’s horn is actually a tooth.”
- **core_fact:** The narwhal’s tusk is an elongated canine tooth with millions of nerve endings, used as a sensor.
- **lane:** WAN
- **visual:** Narwhal surfacing, tusk hero shot, arctic water.
- **risk:** Low.

### 8. starfish_no_brain

- **hook:** “A starfish has no brain. And no blood.”
- **core_fact:** Sea stars have no brain and no blood; they move using a water vascular system that pumps seawater through their bodies.
- **lane:** WAN
- **visual:** Starfish underwater, tube feet moving, slow crawl on rock.
- **risk:** Low.

### 9. platypus_glow

- **hook:** “A platypus glows in the dark.”
- **core_fact:** Platypus fur is biofluorescent, glowing blue-green under ultraviolet light.
- **lane:** WAN
- **visual:** Normal platypus beat, then the same animal glowing blue-green under UV. Striking payoff.
- **risk:** Low. The UV-glow shot is the hero; make that plate the highest-quality one.

### 10. owl_head_turn

- **hook:** “An owl can turn its head almost all the way around.”
- **core_fact:** Owls rotate their heads up to ~270 degrees thanks to extra neck vertebrae and special blood-vessel adaptations.
- **lane:** WAN
- **visual:** Owl doing the head rotation. The motion is the whole video. Perfect for I2V.
- **risk:** Low. This is the most natively-animated topic in the batch.

### 11. cuttlefish_colorblind

- **hook:** “The best color-changer on Earth is colorblind.”
- **core_fact:** Cuttlefish are colorblind yet produce dazzling color and pattern displays across their skin.
- **lane:** WAN
- **visual:** Cuttlefish rippling color waves across its body. Mesmerizing, loopable.
- **risk:** Low.

### 12. seahorse_dad

- **hook:** “In seahorses, the dad gets pregnant.”
- **core_fact:** Male seahorses carry the eggs in a brood pouch and give birth to the young.
- **lane:** WAN
- **visual:** Seahorse with full pouch, then releasing tiny young. Kid-friendly wonder.
- **risk:** Low. Birth-release beat is showable without anatomy cutaway.

### 13. hippo_sunscreen

- **hook:** “Hippos sweat their own sunscreen.”
- **core_fact:** Hippos secrete a reddish fluid (often called blood sweat) that acts as a natural sunscreen and antibiotic.
- **lane:** WAN
- **visual:** Hippo in sun, reddish sheen on skin. Avoid making it look like injury; frame as glistening.
- **risk:** Low-medium. Keep it clearly not-gory; the reddish sheen should read as a coating, not blood.

### 14. koala_fingerprints

- **hook:** “Koala fingerprints look almost exactly like yours.”
- **core_fact:** Koala fingerprints are so similar to human ones they can be hard to tell apart, even under a microscope.
- **lane:** WAN
- **visual:** Koala beat, then macro of the koala’s fingertip ridges. The macro is the payoff.
- **risk:** Low.

### 15. reindeer_eyes

- **hook:** “Reindeer eyes change color in winter.”
- **core_fact:** Reindeer eyes shift from gold in summer to blue in winter, improving low-light vision in the dark arctic season.
- **lane:** WAN
- **visual:** Gold-eye summer beat, blue-eye winter beat. Color shift is the payoff.
- **risk:** Low.

### 16. lyrebird_mimic

- **hook:** “This bird can perfectly copy a chainsaw.”
- **core_fact:** The superb lyrebird mimics complex sounds including chainsaws, camera shutters, and car alarms.
- **lane:** WAN
- **visual:** Lyrebird displaying. **Audio is the hero here** — lean on the George VO plus mimic SFX. Strong loop/replay driver.
- **risk:** Low. This one’s payoff is sonic, so nail the audio design.

## Lane B: Mixed (2) — mostly WAN, one effect beat

### 17. hummingbird_heart

- **hook:** “A hummingbird’s heart beats over 1,200 times a minute.”
- **core_fact:** In flight, some hummingbirds’ hearts beat more than 1,200 times per minute.
- **lane:** MIXED
- **visual:** Hummingbird hover/feed (WAN), plus an optional subtle pulsing-rhythm overlay or audio heartbeat to sell the number.
- **risk:** Low-medium. The number needs a felt beat; use audio/rhythm, not an anatomy cutaway.

### 18. electric_eel_volts

- **hook:** “An electric eel can stun a horse.”
- **core_fact:** Electric eels can discharge up to ~600 volts, enough to incapacitate large animals.
- **lane:** MIXED
- **visual:** Eel (WAN) plus a discharge flash/spark effect beat (light MG/compositing). The shrimp “water flash” lane is a good reference.
- **risk:** Medium. The spark effect is the risk surface; reuse whatever worked on the shrimp flash beat.

## Lane C: MG-test (2) — natural phenomena, use to test the motion-graphics lane

### 19. lightning_hotter_than_sun

- **hook:** “Lightning is five times hotter than the sun.”
- **core_fact:** A lightning bolt can reach ~30,000 K, roughly five times the temperature of the sun’s surface (~5,500 K).
- **lane:** MG
- **visual:** Lightning footage (strong prior, animates well) plus a sun-comparison beat. Phenomenon, not animal.
- **risk:** Medium. The comparison beat is where the MG lane gets tested. Good cohort candidate.

### 20. more_trees_than_stars

- **hook:** “There are more trees on Earth than stars in our galaxy.”
- **core_fact:** Earth has an estimated ~3 trillion trees; the Milky Way holds an estimated 100-400 billion stars.
- **lane:** MG
- **visual:** Forest beat to galaxy beat, scale comparison. Pure MG/scale storytelling.
- **risk:** Medium-high. This is the hardest to make not-feel-like-a-slideshow. Treat as an explicit MG-lane stress test, not a safe-queue filler.

## How to sequence these into cohorts (ties to the kanban)

- **Cohort 1 (safe WAN, anchor the channel):** pull 6-8 from Lane A. These are your highest-confidence bets and should make up the bulk of the next 2 weeks. Tag `cohort: wan-strong-prior`.
- **Cohort 2 (motion-graphics validation):** the 2 MG topics plus the 2 MIXED. Tag `cohort: mg-lane-test`. Publish these spaced out so you can isolate whether the MG lane holds retention the way the WAN lane does. If they underperform the WAN cohort on avg-viewed, you have your answer on where to spend production effort.
- **Vary one thing at a time.** Within the WAN cohort, hold hook style and caption style constant so the only variable is topic. That keeps the retention data interpretable.
- **Front-load the natively-animated ones.** Owl head-turn, cuttlefish color, octopus, and immortal jellyfish need the least motion fakery and will give you the cleanest early reads while you build the queue.
